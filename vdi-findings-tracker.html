Securin · VDI Pre-Launch Audit

VDI Pages — Findings Tracker

Across the three VDI pages, the overall structure is strong: a parent narrative page, a vulnerabilities list page, and a CVE detail page work together to tell the story of a large, enriched vulnerability and threat intelligence corpus. The main gaps are in clarity and trust — the relationship between global VDI data and Securin-generated research isn't explicit, some metrics look inconsistent, and methodology for key concepts is missing or under-explained.

Overall progress

1. Parent VDI Page (Narrative, Stats, Sections)

Strengths

  • Clear high-level narrative: positions VDI as a real-time vulnerability and threat intelligence database with KEVs, zero-days, exploits, attack chains, and an internal research program.
  • Compelling stats and counters that convey scale and depth, including total vulnerabilities, CVEs with exploits, trending CVEs, zero-days, and KEV coverage.
  • Strong sections showcasing Securin Research, zero-day advisories, KEVs, recently discovered vulnerabilities, and KEV Toxic Combo attack chains.
  • Clear commercial CTAs at the bottom: subscribe to Securin threat intelligence and request a demo.

Key Issues

Conflicting zero-day metrics: 2,159 zero-days in one counter, 18 discovered in a research highlight, and 418 discovered plus 202 upcoming advisories elsewhere, with no explanation of how these numbers relate.Open
Blurred lines between global VDI data and Securin-specific research — the page sometimes reads as though everything shown was discovered by Securin, and sometimes as global corpus data.Open
KEV description ("Top CVEs with the highest adversary attention right now") doesn't explain underlying data sources (CISA KEV, dark web, exploit kits, telemetry, incident data).Open
Labels such as KEV, EXPLOIT, RANSOMWARE, TRENDING, ZERO DAY are not defined anywhere on the page, weakening perceived rigor for security practitioners.Open
Toxic Combo attack chains are strong differentiators but currently presented as isolated narratives rather than branded analytic constructs built on VDI and Securin research.Open

Suggested Improvements

Explicitly define VDI in the hero/stats area as the global superset: a vulnerability and threat data lake aggregating public CVE feeds, vendor advisories, community research, and Securin's own discoveries into one normalized corpus.Open
Disentangle zero-day metrics with clear labels, e.g. "2,159 zero-days tracked in VDI (global)," "418 lifetime zero-day findings disclosed by Securin Research," "18 active zero-day cases under coordinated disclosure."Open
Make it explicit in each section whether content is global VDI data or a Securin subset.Open
Add short methodology snippets: define KEV, EXPLOIT, RANSOMWARE, TRENDING, ZERO DAY tags and outline inputs for the Securin Risk Index.Open
Strengthen "Unlock Deeper Threat Intelligence" with practitioner-focused bullets.Open

2. Vulnerabilities List Page (Child / Browse View)

Strengths

  • Successfully conveys breadth: "472,030 vulnerabilities found" immediately communicates that VDI is a large, serious corpus.
  • Filters such as Securin KEVs, Recently Published, Exploits, Trending, CISA KEVs, Zero Days, and Ransomware align well with how vulnerability engineers think about triage and hunting.
  • Clearly pulls from the VDI database and shows concrete CVEs with titles, affected vendors, publication dates, and CVSS scores.
  • Footer CTA reiterates that deeper threat intelligence and the full corpus are available via subscription and demo.

Key Issues

Visually and textually, the page still resembles a generic CVE browser; doesn't immediately communicate how VDI is richer than public NVD or vendor advisory portals.Open
"472,030 vulnerabilities" is stated with no explanation of what makes this corpus special.Open
Filter meanings aren't explained — a first-time visitor doesn't know how "Securin KEVs" differ from "CISA KEVs," or what data drives "Trending" and "Ransomware."Open
Pagination makes clear only a subset of CVEs is shown, but this isn't framed as a deliberate "sample view" for a commercial product.Open

Suggested Improvements

Add a short positioning line near the header explaining this is a live sample of the VDI corpus.Open
Explain filter categories in one sentence each so practitioners understand they're intelligence lenses, not just flags.Open
State explicitly that this view is a limited slice of VDI, and that subscribers get full corpus access, historical data, attack chains, Risk Index, and export/API options.Open
For records with intelligence attributes, visually surface tags such as Exploit, KEV, or Ransomware in the cards.Open
Align CTA copy with practitioner workflows: feeding VDI into VM/detection stacks and exploit-aware prioritization.Open

3. CVE Detail Page (Per-record Depth)

Strengths

  • Clean, practitioner-grade report layout: summary, CVSS, vendor, dates, exposure timeline, exploitability overview, weaknesses, threats, threat actors, MITRE mappings, discussions, references.
  • Exposure timeline, including "Securin Updated" and "CVE Published," shows independent tracking beyond the public CVE publication date.
  • Exploitability Score and Impact Score hint at a proprietary scoring model layered on top of CVSS.
  • Threats, Threat Actors, and MITRE mapping sections remain visible even when empty, signaling these dimensions are tracked and populated where data exists.
  • References linking to vendor documentation (e.g. Quest release notes) provide expected anchors for validation.

Key Issues

The page doesn't explicitly say it's a VDI record enriched beyond public CVE data — enrichment is implied but not stated.Open
Empty fields are presented simply as "No known exploits…" or "No threat actors…", which can read as missing data rather than active monitoring.Open
The relationship between CVSS and Securin Risk Score isn't explained, even though they may diverge for higher-signal CVEs.Open
Commercial tie-back to the broader VDI product is minimal — the page functions mainly as an isolated report view.Open

Suggested Improvements

Add an explicit statement near the top that this is a VDI record combining public CVE information with Securin enrichment.Open
Rephrase empty-field messages to emphasize ongoing monitoring.Open
Include a short explanation of how and why Securin Risk Score can diverge from CVSS.Open
Add a bottom strip tying this record back to the full VDI product, with links to "view more CVEs" and "request a VDI demo."Open
